# Magic [feat. David Archuleta] — Section-by-Section Lighting Design

**Duration**: 3:29 | **Tempo**: 152 BPM | **Key**: C major / G major
**Character**: Pop ballad with building energy arc. Heavily vocal-driven with a prominent instrumental climax in the final third. Harmonic throughout (H/P ratio never drops below 2.0).

---

## Song Arc Overview

```
Energy
  ^
  |                                          ********
  |                                     *****        ***
  |                              *******                **
  |                   **********                         **
  |            *******                                    **
  |     ******                                             *****
  |*****                                                        ****
  +-------------------------------------------------------------------> Time
  0:00  0:14  0:37  1:14  1:28       2:09  2:34        2:59  3:29
  INTRO VERSE1 CHORUS BREAK VERSE2    PEAK  SOLO        TAG   OUTRO
```

The song follows a classic pop arc: gentle intro, vocal verses building through choruses, an instrumental break, a second verse/chorus cycle that pushes to the highest energy at ~2:21, a drums/guitar solo section, a brief vocal tag, and a quiet fade-out. The lighting should mirror this arc — starting intimate and building to a full-house spectacle before pulling back to darkness.

## Section 1: Intro (0:00 - 0:14)

**What you hear**: Synth pad ("other" stem) and piano establish the mood. No vocals, no drums initially. Soft, atmospheric, dark. Guitar is barely present. The "other" stem (synth/pad) is the leader throughout.

**Analysis data**:
- Energy: LOW (-19.8 dB) — quietest section of the song
- Texture: Harmonic (H/P ratio very high — almost no percussion)
- Brightness: Dark (centroid 1882 Hz — low spectral energy)
- Active stems: other, piano, bass, drums (drums very faint)
- Dominant stem: other
- Onset density: 1.5/sec (sparse)

**Lighting approach**: Set the mood. Less is more. The audience should feel anticipation.

**Recommended theme**: **Aurora** or **Stellar Wind** — ethereal, flowing, dreamlike. The slow movement matches the pad-driven texture.

**Tier assignments**:
| Tier | Effect | Why |
|------|--------|-----|
| T1 (Base) | **Color Wash** — slow cycling, deep blues and purples | Establishes ambient atmosphere across the whole house. Dimmed to 30-40%. The pad-like quality of the "other" stem calls for smooth washes. |
| T2 (Geo) | **Twinkle** — gentle random sparkle on front-facing props | Mimics stars or gentle sparkle. Very low density (10-15%). Adds life without drawing focus. (Note: Shimmer is a rapid on/off flash — too aggressive for a calm intro.) |
| T4 (Beat) | Skip or very subtle **Twinkle** | With almost no rhythmic pulse, beat-tier should be inactive or barely perceptible. |
| T6-8 (Props/Hero) | **Off** | No hero moments yet. Keep upper tiers dark to preserve the reveal for when vocals enter. |

**Palette**: Deep blue (#001144), indigo (#220066), soft white (#AABBCC), hint of warm gold (#443300). Dark and cool.

**Key moments**:
- None — this section should be static and calm. No strobes, no impacts. Let the color breathe.

**Value curves**: Brightness should gently ramp from 20% to 40% over the 14 seconds, building subtle anticipation toward the vocal entry.

## Section 2: Verse 1 Opening (0:14 - 0:19)

**What you hear**: Vocals enter at 0:13.7 — the first human voice. The "other" pad continues underneath. Guitar subtly joins. Still building, not full energy yet. Short vocal phrases with gaps between them.

**Analysis data**:
- Energy: LOW (-16.6 dB) — still quiet but 3 dB above intro
- Texture: Harmonic
- Brightness: Neutral (centroid rises to 2305 Hz — vocals bring midrange)
- Vocals: ACTIVE — first vocal entry
- Leader transitions: vocals and piano trading back and forth
- Active stems: all 6 now faintly present

**Lighting approach**: React to the vocal entry. This is the first "moment" of the show — the audience should feel the shift.

**Recommended theme**: Transition from Aurora to **Bio-Lume** or keep Aurora but brighten. The vocal entry should trigger a visible change.

**Tier assignments**:
| Tier | Effect | Why |
|------|--------|-----|
| T1 (Base) | **Color Wash** — warmer tones now, add gold/amber | Shift palette to warmer colors when voice enters. Human voice = warmth. |
| T2 (Geo) | **Ripple** — slow expanding rings from center | The vocal entry is a "ripple" from the center of the song outward. Slow speed (10-15). |
| T4 (Beat) | Light **Bars** or **Single Strand** on roofline | Very gentle movement. Beats are present but subtle — the chase should be slow and understated. |
| T6 (Prop) | **Twinkle** on mini props (stars, ornaments) | Small props come alive with gentle sparkle matching vocal phrasing. |
| T8 (Hero) | Skip | Save hero for the chorus. |

**Palette**: Warm gold (#FFD700), soft amber (#FF8C00), cream (#FFF8E0), hint of blue (#334488). The vocal warmth should shift the palette from cool to warm.

**Key moments**:
- **0:14.1** — Brightness spike (5271 Hz, 3.51 intensity) in the vocals. Trigger a subtle brightness pulse on T1 — a 200ms flash to 60% then back to 45%.
- **0:17.3** — Strongest brightness spike in this section (6199 Hz, 4.65). A brief sparkle burst on the hero prop.

## Section 3: Verse 1 Body (0:19 - 0:37)

**What you hear**: The verse is now fully underway. Vocals are continuous with short phrase gaps. Guitar becomes more present. Energy is building but still mid-range. The song is "breathing" — vocal phrases in, short instrumental fills between them.

**Analysis data**:
- Energy: MEDIUM (-14.8 to -12.9 dB) — steadily climbing
- Texture: Harmonic (vocals dominate)
- Brightness: BRIGHT — centroid jumps to 2800+ Hz
- Onset density: 1.4-2.4/sec — more musical events happening
- Vocals: Continuously active with phrase gaps
- Leader: vocals and "other" alternate rapidly — the pad fills gaps between vocal phrases

**Lighting approach**: Follow the vocal phrasing. Lights should breathe with the singer — brighter during vocal phrases, slightly dimmer during gaps. The breathing pattern is what makes a show feel musical rather than mechanical.

**Recommended theme**: Keep the warm palette. Add more movement.

**Tier assignments**:
| Tier | Effect | Why |
|------|--------|-----|
| T1 (Base) | **Color Wash** with brightness value curve following vocal RMS | Bass wash that literally breathes with the voice. When vocals are loud = brighter, gaps = dimmer. |
| T2 (Geo) | **Wave** — slow sinusoidal, front zones | Flowing movement that follows the melodic contour. Speed 15-20. |
| T4 (Beat) | **Single Strand** chase on roofline at 152 BPM | Beat-synced chase gives rhythmic structure. Half-beat placement so it's felt but not aggressive. |
| T6 (Prop) | **Spirals** on arches, **Marquee** on outlines | Props start contributing visual interest. Slow rotation matching tempo. |
| T7 (Compound) | **Ripple** on matrix/large props | Gentle expanding rings. One per vocal phrase start. |
| T8 (Hero) | Skip until 0:30+ | |

**Palette**: Gold (#FFD700), warm white (#FFF0DD), coral (#FF6B6B), soft lavender (#9B89B3). Building warmth and color variety.

**Key moments**:
- **0:21.1** — Brightness spike + percussive impact simultaneously. Guitar and drums align. Trigger a **Shockwave** on the matrix.
- **0:24.0** — Guitar brightness spike (5.15 intensity — one of the strongest in the song). Flash the hero prop.
- **0:29.5-0.29.8** — Double percussive impact (8.89 + 13.63). Strong drum hits — **Strobe** pulse on beat tier, 2 flashes.

## Section 4: Pre-Chorus / Chorus Build (0:37 - 1:14)

**What you hear**: The song opens up. Bass and drums fully commit. Sub-bass becomes the dominant frequency band (360+ energy, rising to 1600+). Vocals are continuous and more powerful. This is the first chorus cycle — energy doubles from the verse. Multiple sub-sections as the vocal melody moves through different phrases.

**Analysis data**:
- Energy: MEDIUM (-10.5 to -5.3 dB) — significant jump, approaching loud
- Sub-bass: DOMINANT — 278 to 1616 band energy (massive low-end)
- Texture: Harmonic but drums more present (H/P ratio dropping from 3.2 to 2.5)
- Brightness: Alternating bright/neutral — spectral color shifting with vocal melody
- Onset density: Rising from 1.3 to 2.1/sec
- All stems active — full band playing
- Major percussive impacts: 0:44.8 (16.40), 0:41.7 (14.20)

**Lighting approach**: This is the first "big" section. Everything should feel fuller — more props active, faster movement, richer colors. The sub-bass dominance means the bass-driven effects should be prominent (fire height, ripple speed driven by L5 bass curve).

**Recommended theme**: **Inferno** or **Tracer Fire** for aggressive sections, or **Cyber Grid** for structured beat-driven looks. For a pop ballad like this, a warmer approach works better — use an intensified version of the verse palette.

**Tier assignments**:
| Tier | Effect | Why |
|------|--------|-----|
| T1 (Base) | **Color Wash** — now at 50-60% brightness | Full house visible. Rich, saturated palette. |
| T2 (Geo) | **Bars** — horizontal bands moving upward on vertical props | Upward energy matching the building intensity. Speed tied to BPM (38 at 152 BPM). |
| T4 (Beat) | **Single Strand** chase — FULL speed, all roofline groups | Every beat hits. Round-robin across all roofline groups. The chase is now the backbone visual. |
| T6 (Prop) | **Fire** on vertical props (bass-driven height), **Meteors** on arches | Fire height follows the massive sub-bass curve. Meteors on onset triggers from drums stem. |
| T7 (Compound) | **Shockwave** on large fixtures at percussive impacts | The 16.40-intensity drum hit at 0:44.8 should trigger a full shockwave. |
| T8 (Hero) | **Pinwheel** on the showcase fixture | Spinning, beat-synced. Twist angle at 30, speed at 40. Changed at each sub-section boundary. |

**Palette**: Hot coral (#FF4444), electric gold (#FFD700), white (#FFFFFF), deep magenta (#CC0066). Accent palette adds bright cyan (#00FFCC) for punch on T6-8.

**Key moments (prioritized)**:
- **0:43.8** — Energy surge (1.18 intensity) + tempo drop (136 to 99 BPM). This is a micro-breakdown. Brief **Off** (200ms) on all tiers then immediate return = visual "breath" that matches the musical hesitation.
- **0:44.8** — Drums percussive impact (16.40 — strongest so far). **Shockwave** on matrix + **Strobe** flash on all T6+ props.
- **0:45.8** — Energy drop (1.84 intensity — the biggest drop in the first half). Pull brightness down to 30% across all tiers for 500ms. The audience should feel the dip.
- **0:50.7** — Brightness spike (6477 Hz, 4.99 intensity — highest in first half). Full **hero flash** — the showcase prop goes to 100% white for one beat then returns to palette.

## Section 5: Post-Chorus Pullback (1:14 - 1:17)

**What you hear**: Brief transition. Vocals deliver a final phrase then drop out. Energy dips. The bass becomes the dominant stem momentarily. This is the "exhale" after the chorus intensity.

**Analysis data**:
- Energy: MEDIUM (-9.3 dB) — dropped from -5.3 dB
- Dominant stem: bass (vocals handing off)
- Brightness: Dark (centroid drops to 1777 Hz)
- Vocals: Last phrase before 13-second dropout

**Lighting approach**: Quick cooldown. Pull back upper tiers. The visual should "relax" with the music.

| Tier | Effect | Why |
|------|--------|-----|
| T1 (Base) | **Color Wash** dim to 35% | Pulling back |
| T4 (Beat) | Slow chase, half the groups | Reduced activity |
| T6-8 | **Off** or fade out | Upper tiers go dark to set up the instrumental break |

## Section 6: Instrumental Break (1:17 - 1:28)

**What you hear**: NO VOCALS for 13 seconds. The "other" (synth) and piano take over. Drums play an active pattern (onset density jumps to 4.0/sec — the highest in the song so far). Guitar faintly present. This is a musical interlude — the song is "resetting" for the second verse.

**Analysis data**:
- Energy: MEDIUM (-11.5 dB) — moderate
- Vocals: OFF — confirmed silent for full section
- Onset density: 4.0/sec — very busy percussion
- Active stems: drums, bass, guitar, piano, other (NO vocals)
- Leader: other and piano alternate
- Dominant percussive impacts: 18.75 (1:18.3), 15.99 (1:20.9), 15.78 (1:26.9)

**Lighting approach**: This is your chance to do something DIFFERENT. With no vocals, the visual can shift to a completely different character. The high onset density and active drums suggest something rhythmic and pattern-driven. This is the "drum break" section.

**Recommended theme**: **Cyber Grid** or **The Zipper** — mathematical, precise, beat-driven. OR contrast with **Stellar Wind** to create a dreamy interlude.

**Tier assignments**:
| Tier | Effect | Why |
|------|--------|-----|
| T1 (Base) | **Plasma** — slow, cool-toned | Complete palette shift to cool colors. The absence of vocals = absence of warmth. |
| T2 (Geo) | **Bars** — fast, direction-alternating per zone | The 4.0/sec onset density demands fast visual movement. |
| T4 (Beat) | **Meteors** — onset-triggered, falling from top | Every drum hit spawns a meteor. At 4/sec this creates a rain effect. |
| T6 (Prop) | **Spirals** on arches, **Galaxy** on matrices | Cool, spinning, mechanical. No fire — save that warmth for when vocals return. |
| T7 (Compound) | **Shockwave** at major impacts | 18.75-strength drum hit at 1:18.3 gets a full expanding ring. |
| T8 (Hero) | **Pinwheel** — fast spin, cool palette | Continuous spin rather than event-triggered. Speed matches onset density. |

**Palette**: Ice blue (#0088FF), electric cyan (#00FFDD), deep purple (#440088), silver (#CCDDEE). Cool, technical, non-human. The contrast with the warm vocal sections makes the vocal return at 1:28 more impactful.

**Key moments**:
- **1:18.3** — Percussive impact (18.75). Strongest hit in the break. Full **Shockwave** on all large props.
- **1:21.9** — Another strong hit (15.99). Second shockwave, different color from accent palette.
- **1:26.9** — Final strong hit before vocals return. This should be the "trigger" — a brief **strobe** burst that anticipates the vocal re-entry.

## Section 7: Verse 2 + Second Chorus (1:28 - 2:34)

**What you hear**: Vocals return at 1:28 with renewed energy. The second verse/chorus cycle is louder and more intense than the first. Bass and drums are heavier. This is the longest vocal stretch — nearly continuous singing for over a minute. Energy builds steadily from -9.3 dB to the song's peak.

This section has clear internal structure:
- **1:28-1:35**: Verse 2 opening — vocals re-enter, moderate energy
- **1:35-1:51**: Verse 2 body — building, bass dominant, drums intensifying
- **1:51-2:02**: Pre-chorus — energy rising to peak approach
- **2:02-2:09**: Chorus peak approach — near maximum energy
- **2:09-2:34**: PEAK ENERGY — the loudest, most intense section of the entire song

**Analysis data across sub-sections**:
- Energy: MEDIUM to HIGH (-9.3 dB climbing to -3.3 dB)
- Sub-bass: Massive (2000-5000+ band energy)
- Strongest drum hits in the entire song: 25.51 (1:39.3), 26.58 (2:23.3)
- Vocals + drums competing for leader position — rapid transitions
- All stems fully active and loud

**Lighting approach**: This is the BUILD. Every sub-section should feel incrementally bigger than the last. Add tiers progressively. By 2:09, every prop in the house should be active and bright.

### Sub-section: Verse 2 Return (1:28-1:51)

**Tier assignments**:
| Tier | Effect | Why |
|------|--------|-----|
| T1 (Base) | **Color Wash** — warm palette returns immediately | Vocals = warmth. The palette shift from cool (break) to warm (vocals) should be instant and obvious. |
| T2 (Geo) | **Wave** — moderate speed | Smooth movement returns with the melody. |
| T4 (Beat) | **Single Strand** chase — full speed | Beat tier fully re-engaged. |
| T6 (Prop) | **Fire** on vertical props, **Ripple** on horizontal | Fire height driven by bass RMS. The sub-bass is enormous here. |
| T7 (Compound) | **Meteors** — onset-triggered | Building toward compound tier activation. |
| T8 (Hero) | **Butterfly** — slow, mesmerizing | The hero prop starts doing something special but not yet at full intensity. |

### Sub-section: Peak Approach (1:51-2:09)

Now push everything harder:

| Tier | Effect | Why |
|------|--------|-----|
| T1 (Base) | **Color Wash** at 70% brightness | House is lit up. |
| T4 (Beat) | **Bars** — fast, direction changes per bar | More aggressive beat visual. |
| T6 (Prop) | **Shockwave** + **Fire** — alternating per group | Shockwaves expand from drum hits, fire follows bass. |
| T7 (Compound) | **Curtain** reveals at section changes | Dramatic reveals on compound props. |
| T8 (Hero) | **Fireworks** at brightness spikes | Hero prop celebrates the biggest moments. |

### Sub-section: ABSOLUTE PEAK (2:09-2:34)

**This is the climax of the entire song.** Every lighting resource should be deployed.

- Energy: HIGH (-3.3 to -3.9 dB) — maximum, sustained
- Drums: A relentless barrage of strong hits every 1-1.5 seconds from 2:10 to 2:30. Strengths range from 13 to 26 — all above the 98th percentile. There is no single standout hit; this is a sustained plateau of maximum percussive energy.
- Beat RMS stays pinned at 0.40-0.43 continuously — no dips, no breathing room
- Brightness spikes: 4.82 intensity at 2:07.5
- All stems at maximum energy simultaneously

The key insight: **this section is not about individual moments — it's about sustained maximum intensity**. The lighting should reflect that with a continuous wall of energy, not waiting for triggers.

| Tier | Effect | Why |
|------|--------|-----|
| T1 (Base) | **Color Wash** at 80-90% brightness | The whole house BLAZES. Maximum base brightness. Hold it steady — the sustained energy means the base shouldn't flicker. |
| T2 (Geo) | **Bars** — fast, all zones active | Every zone has movement. No dark areas. |
| T4 (Beat) | **Single Strand** chase — tight, continuous, every beat | Not strobe (too fatiguing for 20 seconds). Instead, a fast continuous chase that rides the beat grid. At 152 BPM with hits every beat, this creates a relentless visual pulse. |
| T6 (Prop) | **Shockwave** rotating across groups + **Fire** at max height | With a drum hit every 1.2 seconds, round-robin shockwaves across prop groups — each hit triggers the NEXT group in sequence, creating a visual sweep across the house. Fire height locked at maximum from the sustained bass. |
| T7 (Compound) | **Meteors** — continuous stream, high count (8-10) | Not burst-triggered but continuous rain. The density of hits means individual triggers would blur together anyway. |
| T8 (Hero) | **Pinwheel** or **Spirals** — fast, sustained spin | Continuous rotation at maximum speed. Not event-triggered — the hero prop should just be running full-tilt for the entire 20 seconds. |

**Palette at peak**: Pure white (#FFFFFF), hot pink (#FF0066), electric blue (#0044FF), gold (#FFD700). Maximum contrast. Accent palette goes to white + cyan for the T6-8 punch effects.

**Why sustained effects beat event triggers here**: The drum analysis shows 14 impacts above strength 13 in this 20-second window. If every hit triggered a shockwave on the same prop, they'd overlap and look muddy. Instead:
- Use the **round-robin chase** pattern: hit 1 → group A shockwave, hit 2 → group B shockwave, hit 3 → group C, etc. This distributes the visual energy spatially.
- Keep Fire/Meteors/Spirals running continuously rather than retriggering — the sustained beat plateau means effects should feel like a constant engine running, not individual events.

**Key moments**:
- **2:07.5** — Brightness spike (4.82, full_mix) + vocal spike (3.14). This one IS a standout — it precedes the sustained barrage. Use it as the **launch point**: all upper tiers activate here and then STAY on.
- **2:10-2:30** — The sustained peak. Maintain maximum brightness and activity across all tiers. The visual should feel locked in, driving, relentless — matching the drumming.
- **2:30-2:34** — Energy drops begin cascading (1.13 to 1.35 intensity drops). The barrage ends. Start pulling back T6-T8 effects in preparation for the vocal dropout at 2:34.

## Section 8: Instrumental Solo / Climax (2:34 - 2:59)

**What you hear**: Vocals drop out at 2:34 for a 24-second instrumental section. Drums and guitar take over as leaders. This is the most percussive section — drums dominate with the highest sustained onset density. Guitar plays fills and counter-melodies. The "other" stem (synth) provides atmosphere.

**Analysis data**:
- Energy: HIGH (-2.4 to -2.7 dB) — still at peak energy, just no vocals
- Vocals: OFF for 24 seconds
- Dominant stems: drums, guitar, other (rotating rapidly)
- Leader transitions: extremely rapid — drums/guitar/other trading every 0.5-1s
- Onset density: 3.6-4.1/sec — dense, rhythmic
- Strongest hits: 21.02 (2:49.3), 20.45 (2:50.9), 18.65 (2:43.7)

**Lighting approach**: This is the "guitar solo" section. The visual should shift from vocal-warmth to instrumental-power. The rapid leader transitions between drums/guitar/other create an opportunity for visual "call and response" between different prop groups.

**Recommended theme**: **Tracer Fire** or **Molten Metal** — aggressive, directional, high-contrast. The absence of vocals means the show can be raw and mechanical.

**Tier assignments**:
| Tier | Effect | Why |
|------|--------|-----|
| T1 (Base) | **Plasma** or **Fire** — deep reds and oranges | Aggressive base. The sustained high energy demands an intense foundation. |
| T2 (Geo) | **Bars** — alternating direction per zone, fast | Each zone has independent movement. Creates visual complexity matching the rapid leader transitions. |
| T4 (Beat) | **Meteors** — triggered by drums stem onsets | At 3.6-4.1 onsets/sec, meteors rain down constantly. Use the drums-only onset stream for cleaner timing. |
| T6 (Prop) | **Shockwave** per group, rotating assignment | Round-robin shockwaves across prop groups. Each drum hit triggers the next group in sequence — visual chase across the house. |
| T7 (Compound) | **Pinwheel** — fast spin, accent colors | Continuous spinning at high speed. The mechanical precision matches the instrumental nature. |
| T8 (Hero) | **Galaxy** or **Spirals** — continuous, building | No single moment needs a firework — instead, continuous intense animation. |

**Palette**: Burnt orange (#FF4400), electric red (#FF0000), dark steel (#334455), bright white (#FFFFFF). Accent: hot cyan (#00FFEE) for shockwave rings.

**Special technique — Leader-driven lighting**:
Map the dominant stem at each moment to a prop zone:
- When **drums** lead: roofline chase lights + shockwaves fire
- When **guitar** lead: arches and horizontal props light up with **Wave** or **Spirals**
- When **other** leads: matrix props show **Plasma** or **Butterfly**

This creates a visual representation of which instrument is "speaking" — the audience sees the music move between different parts of the house.

**Key moments**:
- **2:36.5** — Drums impact (18.39). Shockwave expanding from center.
- **2:43.7** — Drums impact (18.65). Shockwave from opposite side.
- **2:49.3** — Drums impact (21.02). Full strobe flash + shockwave.
- **2:50.9** — Drums impact (20.45) immediately after. Double-tap visual — two rapid shockwaves from different origins.

## Section 9: Breakdown / Vocal Tag (2:59 - 3:03)

**What you hear**: Sudden energy collapse. The full band drops away. Vocals return briefly for a final tag/phrase. This is the "falling off a cliff" moment — the energy drops from -2.5 dB to -8.1 dB in under a second.

**Analysis data**:
- Energy: MEDIUM (-8.1 dB) — dropped 6 dB in one second
- Rapid energy drops: 1.87, 1.64, 1.59, 1.54 intensity — cascading collapse
- Vocals: Brief return (3 seconds of singing)
- Active stems: Only drums and vocals (everything else drops out)
- Brightness: BRIGHT — the thin texture means the remaining vocals are spectrally prominent

**Lighting approach**: This is the most dramatic visual transition in the show. Everything should cut. The visual equivalent of the floor dropping out.

| Tier | Effect | Why |
|------|--------|-----|
| T1 (Base) | **Color Wash** — INSTANT drop to 20% brightness, shift to cool blue | The palette should flip from hot to cold in one beat. |
| T2-T5 | **Off** | Kill all middle tiers instantly. |
| T6-T7 | **Off** | Everything dark except base and a single vocal spotlight. |
| T8 (Hero) | **Twinkle** — gentle, on one prop only | A single spotlight-like effect on one prop representing the lone voice. Intimate. |

**Palette**: Pale blue (#88AACC), deep navy (#001133), faint white (#DDDDEE), black (#000000). Nearly monochrome. The warmth is gone.

**Key moments**:
- **2:58.4** — Energy drop (1.44 intensity). THIS is the cut point. All T2-T7 go to Off simultaneously. A 100ms blackout before the base wash fades in at reduced brightness.
- **2:59.2** — Second energy drop (1.87 — the biggest single energy drop in the song). Reinforce the collapse — if any upper tiers were still fading, force them to black.
- **3:02.5** — Brightness spike (4.77 from "other", 3.89 from full_mix). A final sparkle on the vocals — last gasp before the outro.

## Section 10: Outro / Fade (3:03 - 3:29)

**What you hear**: Vocals end at 3:03. The "other" stem (synth pad) carries the song out with occasional drum textures and brief guitar fragments. Energy drops to the lowest point in the song (-24.6 dB). The song dissolves into silence.

**Analysis data**:
- Energy: LOW (-24.6 dB) — quieter than the intro
- Vocals: OFF — gone for good
- Active stems: Only drums (very faint) and other (synth pad)
- Leader: other stem
- Onset density: 0.5/sec — extremely sparse
- Brightness spikes at 3:27-3:28 — brief spectral artifacts near the very end

**Lighting approach**: Mirror the intro but in reverse. Fade everything to black. The show should feel like it's being gently put to sleep.

**Recommended theme**: Return to **Aurora** or **Stellar Wind** — the same ethereal mood as the opening, creating a bookend.

**Tier assignments**:
| Tier | Effect | Why |
|------|--------|-----|
| T1 (Base) | **Color Wash** — same palette as intro (deep blues), fading from 25% to 0% | The show returns to where it started, then dims to nothing. |
| T2 (Geo) | **Twinkle** — very sparse, fading | A few last twinkles like stars going out. |
| T4-T8 | **Off** | All upper tiers stay dark. The show is over except for the base. |

**Palette**: Return to the intro palette. Deep blue (#001144), indigo (#220066), soft white (#AABBCC). Coming full circle.

**Fade strategy**: Apply a brightness value curve that ramps from 25% down to 0% over the full 26 seconds. Don't cut to black — let it dissolve. The last 5 seconds (3:24-3:29) should be nearly imperceptible, with maybe one or two pixels still faintly glowing before true darkness.

## Cross-Cutting Principles

1. **Vocals = Warmth**: Every time vocals are active, the palette should be warm (golds, corals, ambers). Every time vocals drop out, shift cool (blues, cyans, purples). This creates an unconscious association between the human voice and visual warmth.

2. **Bass drives fire**: The sub-bass curve is massive in this song (peaking at 5257 band energy). Any Fire or height-based effect should have its height parameter directly driven by the bass stem RMS via L5 energy curves.

3. **Drums drive impacts**: Use the drums-stem-only onset stream for shockwaves and strobe triggers. This gives cleaner timing than the full-mix onsets which include vocal and guitar transients.

4. **Leader transitions drive spatial movement**: When the leader stem changes (vocals to drums, drums to guitar), the visual focus should shift to a different zone of the house. This creates spatial storytelling.

5. **Reserve 100% brightness for the sustained peak**: The 2:10-2:30 window is a continuous plateau of maximum drum energy (14 hits above strength 13, beat RMS pinned at 0.40+). Don't try to pick a single "flash" moment — instead, hold the full house at maximum brightness for this entire 20-second run, and make sure the sections before it (1:51-2:09) are at 70-80% so there's a perceptible step up when the barrage begins at 2:10.

6. **Bookend the show**: Intro and outro should mirror each other — same theme, same palette, same mood. The audience should feel the story come full circle.
