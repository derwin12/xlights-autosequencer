# Shut Up and Dance

**Artist**: Walk the Moon
**Genre**: Indie pop / dance-rock
**Selected because**: Strong four-on-the-floor beat with a punchy synth-driven arrangement and one of the clearest verse/pre-chorus/chorus/post-chorus structures in the corpus. Beat emphasis is unambiguous, making this a reliable ground-truth track for beat-lock scoring. Jeremy Poling's sequence is widely referenced in the xLights community as a benchmark for prop choreography.

## What the pro did well

- Every chorus hit ("Shut up and dance with me") receives precise beat-lock with a clear effect accent on the down beat
- Pre-chorus build uses progressive tier escalation that arrives exactly at the chorus drop
- Post-chorus instrumental is used as an energy sustain rather than a drop, keeping the audience engaged
- Effect transitions are clean throughout — no awkward mid-phrase cuts

## Caveats

- The sequencer (Jeremy Poling) is known for a prop-heavy layout with many arches and RGB flood groups; the effect choices in the XSQ are tuned to that specific layout and may not generalise to all prop configurations
- Beat grid is very stable (consistent 120 BPM), so any sequencer that tracks tempo correctly should score well on beat-lock for this entry — it is not a difficult test for timing accuracy, but is valuable as a pass/fail sanity check
