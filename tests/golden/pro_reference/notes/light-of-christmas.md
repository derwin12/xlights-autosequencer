# Light of Christmas

**Artist**: Tobymac feat. Owl City
**Genre**: Christmas pop / soft rock
**Selected because**: Wide dynamic range — quiet introspective verse against a full-band uplifting chorus — makes this an excellent test of whether a sequencer modulates tier activation and effect density to match the musical energy curve. Three independent pro sequences allow the harness to measure inter-rater consistency and identify which sequencing choices are near-universal vs. stylistic.

## What the pro did well

- All three sequences treat the first verse as deliberately sparse (lower tiers only), preserving headroom for the chorus payoff
- The Owl City-style "oooh" hook section is consistently identified as a distinct high-energy accent moment
- Palette choices across all three sequences favour warm whites and blues, matching the song's reflective Christmas mood
- The three sequences differ most in how they handle the post-chorus instrumental: one returns to sparse, one sustains energy, one uses it as a transition — useful for calibrating the harness's tolerance for stylistic variation

- Three entries share the same mp3_path and audio_hash; they must each be scored independently despite referencing identical source audio
- The "feat. Owl City" credit can cause filename mismatches in some lookup schemes — the notes_ref is shared across all three pro_ids
