principles
  • One decision surface per section. The brief evaporates into section rows.
  • Algorithms are infrastructure, not UI. Hide 'em until someone opens the drawer.
  • Specialist tools live near the thing they edit, not as siblings in a navbar.
  • Preview early, preview often. A ▷ button on every row short-circuits "generate and check."
  • Errors don't block; they nag. Missing layout becomes a header chip, not a hero banner.
open questions for you
  1. Will xLights users accept a single "vibe" per section, or do they want multi-theme stacking inside one section?
  2. Is "vibe" the right word, or should we call them styles, moods, themes?
  3. How much does the sweep system matter to the hobbyist path? (Today it's a first-class tab.)
  4. Should phoneme editing stay inline or only surface for songs where it actually matters (lyrical holiday songs)?
  5. Is a desktop-app feel (shell + drawers) OK, or do you want this to behave like a web app with deep-links per page?
